The Question

Harvard Athletes play a major role in life at Harvard. Roughly 20% of the student body are athletes, and many more engage in club sports. Make no mistake, being an athelete is a major time commitment. Whether it be team lifts, team runs, team meetings, film study, practice, etc, athletics will find a way to take time up in an athlete's day. This causes many time conflicts with office hours, lectures, and sections. Many of which are either mandatory or not online. This raises the question: Do student-athletes have a larger presence in certain concentrations? If this is the case, is it due to the fact that many athletes have time commitments that interfere with their ability to concentrate in certain concentrations? Being athletes ourselves, this question seems to be one worth answering.



The Framework

We used data from the Harvard Open Data Project which gave us the concentration splits for undergraduate students at Harvard. We then formulated a data scraping algorithm to go on the Harvard athletic websites and retrieve the concentration data for the athletes. We also obtained all the times for concentration-specific classes and over-layed them with the concentration data we retrieved from the athletic websites. Using Charts.js to make an interactive website, we hope to provide the framework one needs to explore and analyze the disparity between the concentrations of student athletes, and the rest of the student body.
