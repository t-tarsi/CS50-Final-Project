The Question

Harvard Athletes play a major role in life at Harvard. Roughly 20% of the student body are athletes, and many more engage in club sports. Make no mistake, being an athelete is a major time commitment. Whether it be team lifts, team runs, team meetings, film study, practice, etc, athletics will find a way to take time up in an athlete's day. This causes many time conflicts with office hours, lectures, and sections. Many of which are either mandatory or not online. This raises the question: Do student-athletes have a larger presence in certain concentrations? If this is the case, is it due to the fact that many athletes have time commitments that interfere with their ability to concentrate in certain concentrations? Being atheletes ourselves, this question seems to be one worth answering.

The Solution

To answer the question, we used data from the Harvard Open Data Project which gave us the concentration splits for undergraduate students at Harvard. We then formulated a data scraping algorithm to go on the Harvard athletic websites and retrieve the concentration data for the athletes. We also obtained all the times for concentration-specific classes and over-layed them with the concentration data we retrieved from the athletic websites. Using D3 to make an interactive website, we hope that one uses our website to answer the question themselves.
